Background

In turn of the recent pandemic, more and more people have been focusing on health and sanitization. One of these aspects that many people practice now in common day lives has been the use of wearing masks in public spaces. Masks are worn to protect the individual and the people around them because of the particles, germs, and bacteria that travel in the air.

Air quality has been a new hot topic that has recently come into light for many people. There are many particles in the air that can not be seen so people do not realize the health risks they expose themselves to. For example, outside, there are particles such as ozone, wildfire smoke (PM 2.5), and nitrogen dioxide particulates that people constantly breathe, where in turn, on extreme levels, people are exposed to respiratory and lung disease. However, the main issue with air is not about what is going on outside around us, but inside buildings. Air particles are concentrated about five times more indoors than outdoors. In fact, there could be more particles found indoors than outside. These particles could come from nearby factories, roads, and traffic density. However, the age of the house, the materials it was built with, occupancy density, pets, and compounds from furniture, kitchen appliances, and dusks can all contribute to the quality of indoor air. With so many variables, this product was designed to help advise people about the air that they breathe and be better informed about what they can do to improve their indoor air quality.
